The summer before my senior year of high school, I designed a power strip that could extend to accommodate bulky plug adapters. Bored in class four years later, I submitted the project to Quirky, a startup that developed products from crowdsourced ideas. Twelve months later, my sketch had become a real, manufactured product, sold at stores like Target, in a box bearing my photo, name, and story. It launched Quirky to “potential unicorn” status, made me their literal posterboy, and spawned a line of spinoffs. I learned a lot about design, startups, and the value of ideas versus execution. Read more in my portfolio and blog post.
