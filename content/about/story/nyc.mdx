After graduation, I followed my friends to Brooklyn. Every block offered a random sample of Earth’s population, and the vibrant chaos of this life expanded my consciousness like the Big Bang. My notion of normalcy was replaced by a humbling, pervasive sense of relativity — the <a target='_blank' href='https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overview_effect'>Overview Effect</a> in a subway car. I learned that I love walking everywhere, that I love breakfast sandwiches. I learned the critical importance of close friends and strong relationships. I began to understand my connection to history, and to deeply appreciate that New York City is the America of America.
